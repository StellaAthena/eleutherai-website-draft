Community

Open collaboration for serious AI research.

EleutherAI began as an open research community and still works through a mix of staff researchers, volunteers, collaborators, and external partners. The community page should help people find the right level of engagement without blurring research standards.

Contribution Map

Find the right place to contribute

The community works best when people enter through concrete research needs: papers, datasets, models, evaluations, implementation work, and discussion that moves projects forward.

Read the research map

Start with the research areas and identify papers, projects, or open questions that match your expertise.

Join project discussion

Use Discord to follow active channels, ask informed questions, and find collaborators around specific work.

Contribute code or analysis

Work on evaluation, data processing, reproducibility, model analysis, or implementation support for public artifacts.

Extend papers

Replicate results, run follow-up experiments, add ablations, or connect EleutherAI artifacts to new research questions.

Build reusable assets

Improve datasets, benchmarks, checkpoints, documentation, and tooling so other researchers can reuse the work.

Support the institution

Provide funding, compute, partnerships, or operational support for open research that requires durability.

Norms

What the community is for

EleutherAI is open and collaborative, but it is not a generic help forum. The strongest contributions come from people who can engage with research-level questions and help move specific work forward.

Research first
