A model suite for studying training over time

Pythia was built for researchers who need controlled access to intermediate states of training, not only final model weights.

Multiple scales

Model families across sizes enable scaling comparisons under a shared training setup.

Intermediate checkpoints

Many checkpoints per model make it possible to study when behaviors and representations appear.

Reproducible setup

Models were designed around public data, released code, and reproducible analysis.

Research substrate

Pythia supports work on evaluation, memorization, circuits, safety, and training dynamics.

Research this project supports

Pythia sits under Training Dynamics, but its checkpoints are useful across evaluation, interpretability, data, and safety research.

Observing behavior across training

Pythia provides checkpoint sequences that make training trajectories visible.

Measuring capabilities over time

Studying learned structure

Checkpointed models support analyses of representation and mechanism development.

Pythia makes training trajectories inspectable.

Most model releases expose final weights. Pythia exposes the path to those weights: intermediate checkpoints, controlled data order, and enough surrounding code and documentation for researchers to ask when capabilities, failures, and mechanisms emerge.
