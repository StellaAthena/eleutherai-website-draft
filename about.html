About

A nonprofit AI research lab focused on interpretability and alignment of large models.

EleutherAI grew from an open research community into a research institute building public artifacts, papers, models, datasets, and tools for studying modern AI systems.

History

From Discord server to research institute

Founded in July 2020 by Connor Leahy, Sid Black, and Leo Gao, EleutherAI began as a Discord server for discussing GPT-3 and became a nonprofit research institute focused on large-scale artificial intelligence research.

Our work has historically focused on providing access to cutting-edge AI technologies by training and releasing models, and promoting open science norms in natural language processing. As public access to large-scale pretrained AI models has improved, our focus has shifted toward researching AI interpretability and alignment.

EleutherAI operates primarily through a public Discord server, where researchers discuss the field and coordinate projects. We embrace an open and collaborative research model, and our community does not strongly differentiate between employees, volunteers, and collaborators at other institutions. The community is oriented toward researchers and research-level discussion.

Mission

Why EleutherAI exists

Large-scale AI research is expensive and technically demanding. EleutherAI's role is to make parts of the scientific record public: artifacts, methods, analyses, and collaboration routes that are not restricted to a few closed labs.

Governance

Decisions about powerful AI should not be restricted to a few companies

Large-scale AI systems are both promising and potentially dangerous. Independent researchers need enough access to study how these systems work, fail, and can be governed.
