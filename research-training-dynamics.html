Training Dynamics

We study how models change over training, and why those changes matter for evaluation, interpretability, safety, and open model development.

View papers Explore directions

Training as an object of study

Understanding a model means understanding the process that produced it: the data, optimization, scale, architecture, and intermediate states that shape its behavior.

Training dynamics treats the training process as a scientific object, not merely a way to obtain a final model. Instead of asking only what a model can do after training is complete, this research area asks how capabilities, failures, representations, and evaluation behavior emerge over time.

For EleutherAI, this area is tied to open checkpoints, reproducible training runs, evaluation infrastructure, and datasets. The goal is to make model development observable: researchers should be able to compare intermediate checkpoints, ask when behaviors appear, and connect those changes to data, scale, architecture, and optimization.

This work supports a more empirical science of language models, where intermediate models, training traces, and evaluation results are public evidence rather than hidden implementation details.

What questions organize this area?

Training dynamics connects empirical measurements to questions about when and why model behavior changes.

Emergence over time

When do capabilities, failures, and internal structure appear during training, and which checkpoints make those changes observable?

Data and optimization

How do data order, filtering, scale, architecture, and optimization shape the path a model takes through training?

Evaluation during training

How should we evaluate intermediate checkpoints, not just final models, so model development becomes easier to understand?
